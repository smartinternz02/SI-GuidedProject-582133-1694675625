Inference:"Speeding" has a moderate positive correlation with "alcohol" (0.6697) and a weak positive correlation with "not_distracted" (0.5880). This suggests that states with higher percentages of speeding drivers also tend to have higher percentages of alcohol-impaired drivers and drivers who were not distracted. "Speeding" has a weak negative correlation with "ins_premium" (-0.0777) and "ins_losses" (-0.0659), indicating that states with higher percentages of speeding drivers tend to have slightly lower car insurance premiums and losses incurred by insurance companies.

"Ins_premium" and "ins_losses" both have weak negative correlations with most other variables. This suggests that states with higher car insurance premiums and losses incurred by insurance companies for collisions tend to have lower percentages of fatal collisions, lower percentages of alcohol-impaired drivers, lower percentages of speeding drivers, and lower percentages of drivers who were not distracted.
